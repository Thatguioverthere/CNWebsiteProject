Outcome 1.7

Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects

There are seven principles on which the GDPR is built on. These principles are:

  • Lawfulness, fairness and transparency
  • Purpose limitation
  • Data minimisation
  • Accuracy
  • Storage limitation
  • Integrity and confidentiality (security)
  • Accountability

[2]. These Principles exist to make sure a consumer knows what is happening with their data and give consent to allow it to be Stored and used by the company, but cannot be used or sold to other companies. The consumer can also consent to have their personal data taken off a companies database.

This personal data can be anything that can identify a person. Most common examples of this are e-mail addresses, passwords and names.[1] The GDPR ensures that this information is used lawfully, making larger companies give power of their personal data back to the user.

The impact of GDPR on digital projects can be still seen today, even since GDPR’s enforcement date back in 2018 [3]. Many companies are still in an implementation phase, meaning that they are still having to build a place to be able to store, collect and use consumer data [4]. Even non-EU companies must comply with GDPR if they have business within the EU [4]. Most of these types of projects also now need an appointed data protection officer or a data controller to ensure the project is fit to run under GDPR [4].

On the Consumer side of GDPR, They must now consent to terms and privacy policy of the company, which gives the company permission to store and use the user’s information and gives the company a timestamp of when this agreement was signed as well. This leaves an audit trail that can later be followed to show that what they opted into, when they did and how they did [4].

In summary, GDPR allows the user to keep track of their own information and allow them to control it. Companies must comply with this and not use a consumer’s information unlawfully. The impact of this means that all infrastructure must be updated and tested to make sure that personal information cannot be used unlawfully and protect against data breaches. This makes the project run for longer, but makes sure the company is protected against fines from the EU union.
